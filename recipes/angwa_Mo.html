Angwa Mo (Oil Rice)

Angwa mo (angwamo, angwa moo or angwamu) is a classic favorite dish of most Ghanaians as it is a
flavorful and quick recipe to follow. Angwa mo is simply rice
fried and cooked in oil. It is one of the easiest dishes you will make.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white rice When using brown or wild rice, soak beforehand.
  • 1 small onion All types of onions work well, except green onion/scallion.
  • 2 cups water I used tap water, but filtered, fluoride-free water is best for health!
  • 2 tbsp oil Vegetable or olive oil (not extra virgin oil due to low burn point! )
    You can use coconut oil if you want it to taste like that.
  • 1 tsp salt Less is more, can add less if you need to.

Preparation

  1. Rinse the rice well and set aside in a bowl
  2. Slice 1 small onion and set aside.
  3. Heat oil on medium heat in a small pot
  4. Add sliced onions and stir-fry until fragrant, golden brown and translucent
  5. Add rice, water, salt, and cover with the foil and lid
  6. Bring rice to boil on high heat (about 2 minutes), then cook on low heat for about 10 minutes
  7. Enjoy
